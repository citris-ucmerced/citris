HackMerced IV

HackMerced is a 36-hour hackathon hosted annually at the University of California Merced. The event features different tracks for hackers to participate and submit their projects in. The Tech for Social Good category is a new initiative added to HackMerced in 2019 that aims to provide a creative space for hackers to create technologically advanced solutions to society's issues.

We also want to recognize the winners from HackMerced IV, a hackathon dedicated to creating a collaborative, interdisciplinary event that brings together students from all universities and prospective sponsors to see the innovation and creativity culminating within the San Joaquin Valley.

Tech for Social Good category:

Winner: Touch the Silent World
"Touch the Silence World is a 2D video game that is coordinated with the leap-motion system. When our team realized the capability of leap-motion on tracking hand movements, we decided to use this system to create a video game that helps the learning of sign language."


 Runner up: Helping Hand
"We as a group are part of a fraternity in which one of are pillars is community service. We want to make an app that would help give back to the community."



For the AgFoodTech category:

Winner: ArtiPet
"We got out inspiration from the old Tamagachi devices that used to be around. We thought it would be a cool idea to bring a more realistic version of that to our modern world."



Runner up: Munch
"Not everyone reads nutrition facts and understands how it affects them. I have been counting calories to lose weight for awhile now and I always find it too difficult to see how different foods affect my day to day nutrition."



Runner up: The Glove
"The desire to provide the necessary equipment to do something good for the community that can be decided upon by the user. The developers have the opportunity to adapt the use of our product across a variety of fields. Unlike other devices, our product is not specific to a singular field with applications from game development to social good. We wanted to create a user interface product that allows the user the freedom to interact with a multitude of devices, of which one pertinent application would be for like a robotic arm for example."
