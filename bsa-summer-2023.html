Bobcat Summer Academy 2023

Drone Workshop at CITRIS

July 10-11, 2023
CITRIS at UC Merced recently hosted an educational 2-day event, the 2023 Bobcat Summer STEM Academy workshop, aimed at teaching high school students the ins and outs of programming self-flying drones. The event saw local high school students dive into the world of drones, exploring them from a technical perspective, while also exploring the real-world applications of drones through discussions, and simulated challenge scenarios. As part of the immersive experience, students were introduced to a curriculum that combined educational videos from familiar faces such as Mark Rober with fun and interactive activities. The activities included exploring 3D digital landscapes generated through drone photogrammetry and wildfire data analysis using satellite imagery. Each of these days ended with competitive challenges that tested the problem-solving skills of the students. The event had a focus on connecting the students’ technical drone skills to real-world scenarios, such as supply delivery during a fire, or a search and rescue mission. The problem-solving challenges would simulate these scenarios and require students to work in teams to accomplish their goals. As the CTIRIS team was getting ready to introduce the first challenge, Joshua Tapia, another instructor for CITRIS Education team, was excited to get going. "These challenges always bring a fun and engaging aspect to these events. The students really start to think outside of the box so they can beat their classmates in these friendly competitions." Kevin Lafreniere, an instructor on the CITRIS Education team, is interested in providing students with experiences they wouldn’t receive elsewhere. "It was fantastic seeing the interest from the students who had never flown or programmed a drone before," he said. "Many of these students wouldn’t consider careers utilizing drones because they haven’t even been exposed to it as an option, so I’m glad we’re able to provide these introductory experiences to them." Under the tutelage of the CITRIS Education team and support from CalTeach, the participants left the event with skills in manual flight routines and hands-on programming experience. The CITRIS team hopes the workshop will serve as a good foundation to progress the students’ interest in their education and aid in building their professional careers.
