The goal of this research is to advance understanding and ability to forecast the effects of wildfires on California’s coastal resources and train the next generation of diverse NASA innovators. This work is funded as a collaboration between NASA’s Ocean Biology and Biogeochemistry (OCEAN) research and NASA’s Minority University Research and Education Project (MUREP) education objectives.

RESEARCH:

KelpFire assesses the effects of wildfire-driven disturbances of carbon and sediment on kelp forests in the California Current System. Remote sensing and in situ observations are coupled with models of watershed exports of sediment and nutrients to the coastal ocean. This multi-disciplinary research will focus on several wildfire impacted watersheds along the California coast.


PhotoCredits: National Marine Sanctuaries

Outcomes

  • Improved understanding of the effect of wildfires on carbon transport from coastal watersheds
  • Information on spatial and temporal patterns of kelp forest change related to sediment & carbon flux
  • New tools to support modelling of carbon flux through coastal aquatic zone and global carbon cycling
